\subsection{Cylinder flow}

\begin{figure}

    \caption{\label{fig:geo-fno-cylinder-result} Representative geo-FNO prediction on the cylinder flow dataset. The panels show the initial velocity field, the ground-truth final velocity field, the model prediction, and the pointwise difference between prediction and ground truth.}
\end{figure}

Geo-FNO was first evaluated on the cylinder flow dataset. Figure~\ref{fig:geo-fno-cylinder-result}
shows a representative prediction on a held-out test sample.

The prediction captures the main structure of the final velocity field,
including the global flow pattern around the cylinder and the downstream
wake. The difference plot shows that the error is not distributed
uniformly across the domain. Larger errors occur primarily in regions
where the velocity field changes more sharply, especially near the wake
and around the obstacle. Despite these local errors, the qualitative
agreement between the prediction and the ground truth indicates that
geo-FNO can learn the velocity-field mapping directly on the irregular
point-cloud representation.

\section{Discussion/Interpretation/Analysis}

This section should connect the non-uniform results back to the role of
geo-FNO as an extension of FNO to irregular spatial data. It should also
make clear that these experiments evaluate performance on held-out
samples, not discretization invariance in the uniform-grid sense.

\subsection{Cylinder flow}

Discuss why the cylinder flow task is a meaningful non-uniform
benchmark. Mention the varying geometry, varying point clouds, and the
fact that the model must predict a vector field on an irregular spatial
domain.

Discuss the error structure in the representative prediction. If the
largest errors occur in the wake or near the cylinder, connect this to
regions with sharper gradients and more complex flow evolution.

Discuss the observation that high-loss samples often correspond to cases
that have not reached a comparable steady or quasi-steady state by the
final time. This can be framed as a dataset limitation rather than only
as a model limitation: the target mapping may be harder to learn when
the final state represents different stages of the transient evolution
for different geometries.

Across the cylinder flow dataset, the model performance is affected by
the fact that not all samples appear to have reached a comparable flow
state by the final time. Samples that are farther from a steady or
quasi-steady state are more difficult to predict, and contribute
disproportionately to the overall error. This makes the reported loss a
measure not only of the model's ability to represent the irregular
geometry, but also of the variability in the temporal evolution present
in the dataset.

\subsection{HTGR column temperature dataset}

The strong performance on this dataset should be interpreted in the
context of the task. The HTGR dataset is smoother and lower-dimensional
than the cylinder flow benchmark: the input consists only of the
boundary temperature and axial level\footnote{As described in appendix \ref{appendix:htgr-input-engineering}, a trigonometric representation of z and T was found to exhibit the lowest loss and was therefore used for the HTGR results reported in the main text.}, and the mesh is shared across all
samples. The result therefore demonstrates that geo-FNO can be applied
to reactor-relevant non-uniform data, but it does not by itself establish
performance on more complex nuclear engineering datasets.

Discuss the feature engineering results if they are important for the
main argument, or refer to Appendix~\ref{appendix:htgr-input-engineering}
if the detailed comparison would distract from the main non-uniform
results.

\subsection{Role of geo-FNO}

Discuss what the non-uniform results do and do not show. They show that
geo-FNO can be applied directly to irregular spatial representations and
produce meaningful predictions on held-out data. They do not show
discretization invariance in the same sense as the uniform-grid
experiments, because no systematic variation in point-cloud density or
mesh resolution was tested.
