\section{Contributions and Conclusions}

showed that CNO is more robust to resolution changes than FNO, and connected this to the bandlimited assumption and the way that Fourier truncation does not preserve the same physical meaning across resolutions. Showed that geo-FNO can be applied to non-uniform meshes and performs well on the irregular-domain datasets, but also identified a seam artifact that appears across datasets and can corrupt low-$k_x$ modes. Showed that geo-FNO can be applied to nuclear datasets.

\section{Future work}

talk about possible improvements, shortcomings, what I would have done differently knowing what i know now etc.

to mention:

\begin{itemize}
    \item I did not investigate "discretization invariance" for non uniform meshes. here, this would mean something like worse sampling, fewer data points, or a subsampled section of the domain. i will have explained in the thesis that standard discretization invariance is not really applicable to non-uniform meshes, but I could have done some experiments to see how the models perform under these conditions.
    \item I would have liked to have included another architecture for non uniform meshes. for uniform grids, having the global FNO and local CNO proved to be a good contrast, but for non uniform meshes, I only had the geo-FNO. it would have been interesting to have a local architecture for non uniform meshes as well, to see if the same trends hold as for uniform meshes. This especially holds for the cylinder flow dataset, as a local model is likely to perform better than the geo fno on such a dataset.
    \item I would have liked to have performed more actual experiments for non uniform meshes. For example to compare geo fno to interpolating to a regular grid and using FNO, or to compare geo fno to a local architecture on the cylinder flow dataset. I would have also liked to have done some experiments to investigate the seam artifact that appears in geo fno predictions, and see if this can be mitigated through architectural changes or training strategies.
    \item I would have liked to try to make a geo-CNO. I imagine taking the coordinate transform IPHI from geo-FNO, producing a regular grid and feeding that into a CNO. This could then already include the resampling onto a fixed internal grid. This would be interesting to see if the bandlimited assumption can also help stabilize learning on non uniform meshes, and if the local inductive bias of CNO can help mitigate the seam artifact that appears in geo-FNO. This was also mentioned in the CNO paper as a potential future direction, but I did not have time to explore it.
    \item The fact that the HTGR dataset is not very challenging, and how a more complex dataset could be constructed to better test the capabilities of the models.
\end{itemize}
